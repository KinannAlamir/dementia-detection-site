The Challenge

Diagnosing a disease instead of preventing a crisis.

Too Late

Today, we diagnose dementia when patients can't remember their grandchildren's names. On average, diagnosis happens 7-10 years AFTER neurological decline begins.

Irreversible

By the time symptoms are visible, the damage is often irreversible. We are missing the critical window for effective intervention.

The Cost

Millions of people are affected, costing billions in care. We're not lacking treatments—we're lacking prediction.

Our Solution

AI-Powered Prediction

"We've built an AI-powered algorithm that predicts dementia risk 5-10 years before clinical symptoms appear."

Our technology uses data that already exists in medical records to flag risk early.

With our algorithm in standard screening, at a fraction of the cost of current solutions, patients could be flagged years earlier—while interventions still matter, while they can still plan, while they can still be themselves.
